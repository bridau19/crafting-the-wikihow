The Official Macaron Troubleshooting Guide

This guide will explain how to troubleshoot macarons when everything is going wrong, because a lot can go wrong when baking macarons. Many of the same causes can lead to very different issues. Macarons come out best when your meringue is strong, batter is perfectly macaronaged, shells are evenly piped, and they are baked at the right temperture. Not hard, right?

Problem Solution
Bulging feet
  • Too high oven temperature
  • Over-mixed batter

    • Bulging feet isn't the worst issue to have, but some people may not like the look of it.
    Rough, grainy, dull or has points on top
  • Almond flour is too course
  • Under-macaronaged

    • Macarons that are properly macaronaged and made with fine ground almond flour will have smooth, shiny tops.
    Flat, spread out shells
  • Almond flour is too course
  • Over-mixed

    • If your batter is too loose, the macarons will spread out to be very thin.
    No feet
  • Weak meringue or over-mixed
  • Low oven temperature
  • Too much humidity
  • Shells rested too long or not long enough

    • In humid climates, it may be helpful to use aged egg whites, as older egg whites lose some moisture. Make sure the oven is hot enough for air bubbles in the meringue to expand and lift the shells.
    Lopsided shells
  • Uneven circulation in oven
  • Convection fan is too strong
  • Improper piping technique

    • Make sure to pipe the macarons evenly, perpendicular to the mat, and make sure they dry and bake evenly.
    Hollow shells
  • Too high oven temperature
  • Under-baked or over-mixed
  • Meringue technique issue
  • Not matured

    • It's ok if macarons have a small gap at the top because once you fill them and let them mature in the over for 24 hours, the shells will magically fill!
